Description

Consider trying the natural way of making tartar sauce, this has the french origin and taste exactly how you would love it, the sauce blends with varieties such as garlic bread, seafood, yam, sterk, chicken and whatever works for you.

  1. 2 small dill pickles
  2. 1tbsp of capers
  3. 1tbsp of dried dill
  4. 1tbsp of chopped chives
  5. 1tbsp of dried parsley
  6. 1tbsp fresh lemon juice
  7. 1tbsp cayenne pepper
  8. Pinch of fresh ground black pepper
    9. Home-made Mayonnaise
  9. 2 egg yolks
  10. pinch of salt
  11. 25cl (1 cup) of olive oil
  12. 1tbsp of vinegar (wine or apple cider)
  1. Chop more or less finely (to your taste) the capers and pickles
  2. Mix in the chives, dill, parsley, cayenne pepper, lemon juice and black pepper to make a tartar base
  3. Optional: Make a mayonnaise or use store-bought. To make the mayonnaise, whisk the egg yolks in a owl. Then combine in the olive oil, vinegar, and salt until smooth.
  4. Mix in the tartar base into the mayonnaise
  5. Set aside in fridge once the tartar is smooth, and serve when ready.
